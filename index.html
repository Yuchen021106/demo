Adaptive and High-Fidelity Matching Algorithm for Zero-Shot Accent Conversion

Xinran Wang1, Ning chen1, Yixin luo1, HongqingZhu1, Li Tian1, Zhiying Zhu1

1East China University of Science and Technology

Abstract

Zero-shot accent conversion (AC) aims to transform accent-related acoustic characteristics of the source speech to a target accent while preserving linguistic content and speaker identity. Unlike conventional parametric generation-based AC models, the retrieval-based ones, which employ k-nearest neighbors (kNN) to directly retrieve acoustic features from target-accent speech and so do not need source-accent speech during training, are more effective. However, existing retrieval-based AC frameworks are limited by three fundamental issues: the limited intelligibility of the converted speech resulted from the incomplete phoneme coverage achieved by sequential selection-based matching pool construction, the limited adaptability to the variations in the source speech content, and low spectral continuity resulted from coarse-grained style modulation. To address these limitations, we propose AA-kNN-AC, an enhanced retrieval-based AC model. First, we design a phoneme coverage-based matching pool construction strategy, employing greedy selection with diversity-aware scoring to achieve balanced and near-complete phonetic representation under limited target accent data. Second, we propose an anisotropy-aware adaptive kNN retrieval mechanism that dynamically determines the neighborhood size according to the singular value decomposition-based anisotropy analysis, enhancing its adaptability to the variations in the source speech. Third, we present a progressive AdaIN-based fine-grained decoder by integrating temporal up-sampling within Adaptive Instance Normalization (AdaIN) blocks, enhancing spectral continuity of the converted speech. Experimental results on the L2-ARCTIC and VCTK datasets demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms existing retrieval-based and generative baselines in both objective and subjective evaluations, achieving high intelligibility, speaker similarity, and naturalness under zero-shot scenario.
